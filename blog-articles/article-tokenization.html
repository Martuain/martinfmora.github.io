Tokenization is the process of converting rights to a real-world asset into a digital token on a blockchain network. It enables fractional ownership, increased liquidity, and borderless trading of traditionally illiquid assets like real estate. While early applications emphasized property markets, the opportunity now spans financial instruments, commodities, IP, and more.

How Tokenization Works

Breaking down the tokenization process

1

Asset Selection

  • Identify and evaluate the underlying asset (real estate, commodities, artwork, IP, financial instruments).
  • Consider asset valuation, liquidity potential, regulatory compliance, and market demand.
  • Assess legal ownership, transferability rights, and any encumbrances.
2

Legal Structure

  • Create a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) or trust to hold the asset.
  • Ensure compliance with securities laws and obtain regulatory approvals.
  • Define token-holder rights (voting, profit-sharing, exit strategies).
3

Token Creation

  • Select blockchain platform (Ethereum, Polygon, or specialized systems).
  • Define token standards (ERC-20, ERC-721, etc.).
  • Program smart contracts for governance and asset management.
  • Implement security (multi-sig wallets, audits).
4

Distribution

  • Distribute tokens via ITOs, private placements, or direct sales.
  • Implement KYC/AML and investor accreditation checks.
  • Run marketing/education campaigns and transfer tokens to investor wallets.
  • Manage phased distribution to support price stability.
5

Trading

  • Enable secondary trading on exchanges, DEXs, or token platforms.
  • Maintain compliance monitoring and regular reporting.
  • Support liquidity through market-making activities.
  • Respect transfer restrictions, lock-ups, and regulatory rules.

Each stage requires coordination between legal experts, blockchain developers, compliance specialists, and financial professionals to ensure successful tokenization that benefits both asset owners and investors.
