Case Study 01

From reconciliation tool
to data governance platform

Revenue + Architecture Data Governance SaaS · Southern Europe 2025 — Ongoing

The Situation

A technically strong automated reconciliation platform — 300+ active users, 16M+ monthly transactions, 95%+ automation rate — was preparing to enter Spain and Europe from its home market. The product worked. The structural problem was positioning.

Entering Spain as a "bank reconciliation tool" meant competing on price against accounting ERPs that offered basic reconciliation as a feature. That positioning destroyed the margin the product deserved.

The G.E.A.R. diagnosis identified two compounding gaps: a Revenue gap (priced as a utility, operating as infrastructure) and an Architecture gap (the narrative led with the accounting use case, not the data operations capability the platform actually had).

What I Did

Full mapping of the Spanish payment ecosystem — identified PSPs and payment orchestrators as the primary acquisition segment, the only buyers experiencing reconciliation pain at the scale the platform was built for
Regulatory research into PSD2/PISP/AISP obligations for Spanish payment providers — built a positioning bridge making the platform's compliance data capabilities a selling point, not a footnote
Led the strategic pivot from reconciliation SaaS to data governance platform — re-framing existing capabilities (lineage, enrichment, transformation, governance automation) into a higher-value category without changing the product
Designed Q1–Q3 AI/ML product roadmap using RICE/WSJF prioritization, projecting 40% customer retention increase

Case Study 02

Web3 architecture for
a Gen-Z narrative platform

Architecture + Expansion Web3 · Consumer Platform Sep 2025

The Situation

A consumer platform built around collaborative short-form narrative (the "6 words" format) targeting Gen Z users aged 17–28 came to me as independent Tech Advisor. Strong cultural insight, clear audience — but the technical vision was significantly more complex than the validation stage warranted.

The founding team's instinct was to build toward the end-state: blockchain-native, token-incentivized, NFT-enabled. Building that architecture before validating the core engagement hypothesis would have created serious structural debt and slowed the critical early feedback loops.

What I Did

Designed and facilitated a 5-day Design Sprint (Sep 8–12, 2025) to validate the core engagement hypothesis before any significant technical investment — concept scored 90% in user testing, 30-day retention potential revised upward to 65% with UX fixes applied
Identified the real resonance driver: not gamification or monetization, but the social purpose dimension — creative participation around cultural references. This insight led the product narrative going forward
Delivered a phased MVP architecture: Next.js / Node.js / PostgreSQL core (no blockchain) → social layer if Phase 1 data warranted → Polygon integration only when retention metrics justified the architectural complexity
