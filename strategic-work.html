Case Studies

Where G.E.A.R. has moved the needle

Each engagement starts with a structural diagnosis. What follows is a focused intervention — governance redesign, revenue repositioning, or architectural realignment — that removes the constraint blocking growth.

01
Governance
Payments · Latin America

From reactive compliance to regulatory moat

A B2B payments platform operating across five Latin American jurisdictions was spending significant engineering capacity on compliance as a cost center — patching regulatory gaps reactively, country by country.

The structural diagnosis revealed a deeper issue: governance was decentralized by accident, not design. Each local entity had independent decision rights with no common framework, creating duplication, blind spots and friction for enterprise buyers.

Structural Outcome

Redesigned governance architecture with a unified compliance layer. Reduced regulatory response time and repositioned the compliance function as a trust signal in enterprise sales — shortening average deal cycle significantly.

02
Revenue
Data Platform · Europe

Escaping the tool trap through pricing restructure

A data intelligence platform for financial institutions was consistently losing to cheaper point solutions — not because their product was inferior, but because their pricing model communicated the wrong value tier.

The platform was priced and packaged as a workflow tool when it was actually functioning as strategic infrastructure for its best clients. The structural gap was between the value being delivered and the value being claimed.

Structural Outcome

Repositioned from tool to infrastructure with a tiered model anchored to business outcomes. Enabled premium pricing conversations with strategic buyers and reduced competitive surface area against point solution vendors.

Revenue ArchitecturePricing StrategyPlatform PositioningB2B SaaS
03
Architecture
Web3 · Global

Structural realignment for institutional-grade Web3

A Web3 infrastructure provider targeting institutional clients had strong technology but faced repeated friction in sales cycles. Buyers — traditional financial institutions — couldn't map the platform's architecture to their own risk and governance frameworks.

The platform spoke in protocol language. Institutional buyers spoke in operational risk, counterparty exposure and audit trail. The structural gap was in translation, not technology.

Structural Outcome

Redesigned platform narrative and technical documentation architecture to align with institutional due diligence frameworks. Created a governance overlay that mapped Web3 concepts to traditional finance compliance vocabulary.
