Fintech · Strategic Repositioning

From Commodity Tool to Strategic Advantage: 3x Revenue in 12 Months

A Latin American reconciliation SaaS was competing on price in a crowded market. We repositioned them as compliance infrastructure—and transformed their business model overnight.

The Situation

Early 2025. A Montevideo-based fintech had built technically solid reconciliation software serving 15 payment service providers across Spain and Portugal.

€50M Monthly transaction volume processed
99.7% Reconciliation accuracy rate
€240K Annual recurring revenue
35% Annual churn rate

While venture-backed competitors raised millions and burned cash on customer acquisition, this bootstrapped team was stuck in a price war they couldn't win—competing against 50+ similar tools at €300-500/month.

Translation: They were slowly dying, one churned customer at a time.

The Discovery Insight

During discovery with their largest client (a PSP processing €2M daily), the CFO revealed everything:

Last year we had a €120,000 fine because we couldn't trace a settlement discrepancy across four bank accounts fast enough for the auditor.

That was the moment. They weren't solving "reconciliation"—they were solving:

  • PSD2 regulatory compliance (€50K-500K in potential fines)
  • Multi-bank treasury management (operational efficiency)
  • Settlement visibility across payment networks (risk management)
  • Audit trail creation (fintech license requirements)

But they were selling it as "automated reconciliation for €300/month."

The strategic gap: Positioning as a commodity feature instead of compliance infrastructure.

The Approach

RICE framework, 90% roadmap delivery

New messaging, €4.2K avg pricing

6 enterprise clients, €720K ARR

Complete repositioning executed:

  • Product name: ReconcilePro → PaymentControl Platform
  • Pricing: €300/mo flat → €2,000-8,000/mo value-based
  • Target: "Companies needing reconciliation" → "EU-regulated PSPs"
  • Message: Feature lists → "Avoid €200K in PSD2 fines"

RICE prioritization delivered: Multi-entity consolidation, real-time dashboards, audit-ready trails, and anomaly detection—90%+ on schedule.

The Results

Annual Revenue €240K €720K 3x growth
Sales Cycle 6 months 2 months 67% faster
Annual Churn 35% 8% 77% improvement
Competition 50+ tools 3 platforms Strategic position

CEO reflection: We were technically excellent but strategically invisible. The repositioning didn't change what we built—it changed how the market perceived our value. We stopped competing on price because we stopped being compared to reconciliation tools.

Strategic Lesson

Market position isn't defined by what your product does—it's defined by the strategic problem your buyers lose sleep over. We stopped selling 'automation' and started selling 'regulatory peace of mind.' Everything changed.

Web3 · Product Validation

Can Gen Z Create Movies with 6 Words? This Startup Got 100% Buy Intent

A founder with a 20-year-old idea needed validation: Would audiences pay to co-create entertainment? We ran a 5-day sprint and proved the concept—then fixed the execution disasters that almost killed it.

The Situation

September 2025. A film producer approached with an ambitious concept refined over 20 years:

What if audiences could fund AND create movies together, starting from just 6 words?

The pitch involved "Dream Drops" (symbolic NFTs), "Cosmos" (shared creative universes), collaborative storytelling with "6-word contributions," and blockchain technology. It was compelling but confusing.

The challenge: Validate if this concept could work BEFORE building expensive blockchain infrastructure.

20 years Idea refinement with zero validation
5 days Design Sprint timeline
$500 Prototype budget (vs $50K+ blockchain)
Gen Z Target audience (17-28 years)

The Problem

Three critical issues emerged during discovery:

1. Confusing Value Proposition

  • What IS a "Cosmos"? What IS "Lore"?
  • Why would I pay for 6 words when I can write for free?
  • Is this storytelling, crowdfunding, or NFT speculation?

2. Technology-First Thinking

Original plan: Full blockchain, smart contracts, token economics, multi-wallet support, complex gamification.

Our question: Do we need blockchain to validate if people want this?

3. No Proof of Demand

20 years of iteration. Zero users. Zero transactions. Zero validation.

The real risk: Build a $50K+ blockchain platform that nobody wants.

The Approach

We stripped the idea to its essence:

"A decentralized entertainment financing platform leveraging blockchain technology to enable audience participation in creative production through tokenized contribution mechanisms"

↓

"Buy a $15 Dream Drop. Get 6 words from a creator. Add your 6 words. Watch it become a movie."

Reference point: Hemingway's famous 6-word story: "For sale: baby shoes, never worn."

If 6 words can tell a complete story, could they be the seed for collaborative entertainment?

D1-2
Simplify
Core value prop definition
D3-4
Prototype
Wizard of Oz MVP ($500)
D5
Test
3 Gen Z users, 20 min each

Instead of blockchain, we built:

  • Simple web app (Next.js) with simulated tokens
  • Manual curation (no algorithms)
  • Discord for community (not custom chat)
  • Like/share functionality
  • Character profiles (hardcoded for testing)

Total dev time: 48 hours

The Moment of Truth

Testing with 3 Gen Z users (ages 19-24):

100% Purchase intent (almost impossible to achieve)
90% Confusion rate (execution problem)
3/3 Users validated core concept

The Good News:

  • All 3 users said they would buy a Dream Drop
  • Strong engagement with the 6-word contribution feature
  • Clear differentiation from existing platforms
  • Resonated with Gen Z's desire to participate in creation

User feedback: "This is actually really interesting. It's like being part of the story from day one, not just watching it later. I love that I can contribute something small but meaningful—it's not overwhelming like 'write a whole script.'"

The Bad News (Execution Problems):

Despite 100% buy intent, every user was confused:

  • Intro video didn't explain the platform connection
  • Unclear what a "Lore" was, couldn't tell fiction from reality
  • Broken interactions (clicked "View Full" → nothing happened)
  • Visual monotony, weak CTAs, looked "too fintech-y"

Critical user insight: "I love the idea, but the execution makes me think you're not ready. It looks like a PowerPoint, not a product."

The verdict: Concept validated. Execution broken.

The Results

Metric Before Sprint After Sprint
Concept Validation 0% (no users tested) 100% buy intent
Time to Validation 20 years (no data) 5 days
Investment Required $50K+ (blockchain build) $500 (prototype)
User Confusion Unknown 90% (fixable)
Execution Quality N/A 3/10 (needs work)

Updated probability assessment:

90% Concept validation (confirmed by testing)
65% 30-day retention (with UX fixes)
55% 6-month sustainability (execution dependent)
75% Scale potential (validated demand + tech path)

Founder reflection: After 20 years with this idea, the sprint confirmed I wasn't crazy. The concept works. Now I just need to execute it properly. For the first time, I have real data, not just intuition.

What we prevented:

  • $50,000+ blockchain build without validation
  • 6-12 months of development before learning
  • Feature creep without user feedback
  • Building for the wrong audience

What we enabled:

  • Validated concept in 5 days vs 6 months
  • Clear roadmap based on real user feedback
  • Phased approach reducing financial risk
  • Investor pitch with actual data ("100% buy intent")
Strategic Lesson

The hardest part of innovation isn't building technology—it's validating that anyone wants it. We proved the concept had legs before writing a single smart contract. The execution problems are 100% fixable. The demand validation is priceless. Validate demand before building infrastructure. Always.
